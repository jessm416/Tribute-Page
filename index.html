Marie Curie

The only person to win the Nobel Prize in two scientific fields

Madame Curie at work in her lab

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw, Congress Poland, Russian Empire
  • 1891 - Becomes a student at the Sorbonne
  • 1895 - Marries Pierre Curie
  • 1898 - Coins the term "radioactivity"
  • 1903 - Shares the Nobel Prize in physics with her husband
  • 1906 - Becomes the first woman professor at the Sorbonne
  • 1911 - Wins the Nobel Prize in chemistry for her discovery of the elements polonium and radium
  • 1914 - Developed mobile x-ray equipment for patient treatment in hospitals during World War I
  • 1920 - Founds The Radium Institute in Paris
  • 1921 - Recieves 1 gram of radium donated by the United States to The Radium Institute
  • 1932 - Opens The Radium Institute in Warsaw
  • 1934 - Dies at age 66 from lifetime exposure to radiation
